My Life, By AI – Landing Page Brief (Audience-Aligned)

1. Purpose

Design a landing page that speaks **directly** to people who have tried to change their lives many times, failed repeatedly, and are now curious whether AI + structure could finally make change sustainable.

The page should:

  • Make Serial Restarters feel immediately **seen** and **safe**.
  • Explain that this is a **ChatGPT life coach documentary + systems library**, not just AI feature demos.
  • Point them clearly to **where to start on YouTube**.
  • Set expectations that failure is part of the story and the system.

Primary CTA: **Start the documentary on YouTube**
Channel: https://www.youtube.com/@MyLifeByAI

2. Audience (On-Page Framing)

We are talking to:

  • People who have tried 5–20+ diets or self‑help systems and "failed" them.
  • People with ADHD, anxiety, depression, or chronic illness who feel overwhelmed and ashamed.
  • People who can’t afford a coach or nutritionist but still want structure and accountability.
  • People who binge your content because they’re building courage to try.

They are not primarily "AI nerds" – they’re **struggling transformers** who see ChatGPT as:

  • A non‑judgmental human stand‑in.
  • A way to remove decisions and build routines.
  • A system that might finally be different from all the others.

The page must **acknowledge their history of failure and shame** without triggering it further.

3. Page Goals (V1)

By the time someone scrolls to the bottom, they should:

  • Know **this is for people like them** (serial restarters, weight‑loss desperates, ADHD organizers).
  • Understand the project as a **real‑time documentary + practical systems**, not hype.
  • Trust that you will show **failures and comebacks**, not just highlight reels.
  • Believe that the systems are **replicable and affordable** (no high‑ticket coaching required).
  • Feel clear that the **next step** is to start watching the documentary on YouTube.

4. Page Structure & Copy

4.1 Hero Section

**Goal**
Make the right person think, "Oh. This is literally about me." within 3–5 seconds.

**Elements**

  • Project name.
  • Subheading that names the audience and their pattern.
  • Primary CTA button to the YouTube channel.
  • Supporting line that normalizes failure and frames AI as a non‑judgmental partner.

**Draft copy**

  • Title:

    • `My Life, By AI`

  • Subtitle:

    • `A real-time documentary of someone who failed at diets and self-help for years, rebuilding life with a non-judgmental AI coach.`

  • Supporting line:

    • `If you've tried everything and still feel stuck, this project is for you.`

  • Primary button:
  • Label: `Start the documentary on YouTube`
  • Link: `https://www.youtube.com/@MyLifeByAI`
  • Secondary text link (under hero):
  • Label: `Or learn what this is all about`
  • Scrolls to the “What this project is” section.

Tone: calm, honest, non‑hype, no shame.

4.2 What This Project Is

**Goal**
Define the project in one clear paragraph that matches how viewers talk about it: "ChatGPT as my life coach".

**Draft copy**

Heading:
`What is “My Life, By AI”?`

Body:

"My Life, By AI" is an ongoing experiment in letting ChatGPT act as my life coach, project manager, and accountability partner.
I’ve failed at diets, routines, and self-help more times than I can count. Instead of hiding that, I document it. Together with my AI coach (Arthur), I’m rebuilding my life using systems, not willpower — and showing you everything, including the relapses.

Key bullets (optional):

  • A **vlogumentary**: real‑time documentary + systems + simple science.
  • For people who have **tried everything and are tired of failing alone**.
  • Built around the idea that **failure is data**, not a dead end.

4.3 Who This Is For

**Goal**
Let visitors self‑identify quickly and feel seen.

**Draft copy**

Heading:
`If this sounds like you, you’re in the right place`

Bullets:

  • You’ve tried diets, routines, and planners for years — and you’re exhausted by the cycle of restart → crash → guilt.
  • You live with ADHD, anxiety, depression, chronic pain, or other conditions that make "just try harder" advice useless.
  • You want structure and accountability but **can’t afford** a coach, trainer, or therapist for everything.
  • You’re curious whether an AI you can talk to in private could be the missing piece.

Short reassurance line:

You’re not broken and you’re not alone. You just haven’t had a system built for how your brain and life actually work.

**Goal**
Make it obvious that the documentary lives on YouTube and is the next step.

**Draft copy**

The main story happens on YouTube. That’s where you’ll see every weigh‑in, every relapse, every system tweak, and every win — in real time.

Button (repeat CTA):

  • Label: `Start watching on YouTube`

Optional supporting text:

If you’re not ready to start yet, you can just watch. A lot of people do that first.

**Goal**
Explain why they should care about this website if the content is on YouTube.

`Why this site exists`

YouTube is great for watching the journey, but terrible for keeping systems, prompts, and resources organized.
This site is the home base for "My Life, By AI" — a place to collect the exact systems I use, the prompts you can copy, and the behind‑the‑scenes notes that don’t fit in a video description.

Optional bullets:

  • Find **where to start** in the documentary, based on your situation.
  • Get **written breakdowns** of key systems and episodes.
  • Explore **condition‑specific guides** (ADHD, insulin resistance, chronic pain, etc.) as they’re released.

**Goal**

**Draft copy**

Body:

This is the first version of the site. Over time, it will grow into a hub for people who want to build their own AI‑powered Life OS.

5. Visual & UX Notes

  • Overall feel: **clean, calm, non‑intimidating**.
  • Layout:
  • Simple, mostly single‑column layout with clear sections.
  • Plenty of whitespace so overwhelmed visitors don’t feel visually stressed.
  • Colors:
  • Soft, approachable palette (not harsh reds/oranges associated with urgency or danger).
  • One or two accent colors for CTAs.
  • Typography:
  • Clear, readable sans‑serif.
  • Strong hierarchy: large, friendly headlines; short paragraphs.
  • Mobile‑first:
  • Primary CTA visible above the fold on mobile.
  • Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and clear buttons.

6. Implementation Notes (for Dev)

  • Framework: Next.js (React + TypeScript).
  • Entry page: `app/page.tsx` or `pages/index.tsx` as the main landing page.

Recommended metadata:

  • Title:

    • `My Life, By AI – A ChatGPT Life Coach Documentary for People Who’ve Tried Everything`

  • Meta description:

    • `For people who’ve failed diets and self-help plans over and over, My Life, By AI is a real-time documentary of using ChatGPT as a non-judgmental life coach to rebuild health, work, and habits with systems—not willpower.`

  • Open Graph:
  • OG title similar to page title.
  • OG description similar to meta description.
  • OG image: simple graphic with project name and tagline (no heavy branding needed).

7. Success Criteria (V1)

We’ll consider the v1 landing page successful if:

  • Serial Restarters land on the page and think, **"This is me"** within a few seconds.
  • Visitors understand that this is a **documented, messy, real transformation**, not a polished highlight reel.
  • The YouTube CTA is clear and gets clicked by people who resonate.
  • The page sets accurate expectations: systems + science + story, with failure included.
  • Comments and messages begin to echo language from the page ("I’ve tried everything", "this makes me feel less alone").
